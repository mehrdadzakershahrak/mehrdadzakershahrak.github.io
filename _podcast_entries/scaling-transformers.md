The R2L framework differs from traditional reinforcement learning (RL) primarily by reframing reasoning as a structured compression algorithm rather than just a sequence of logical steps. While traditional RL often requires experience to narrow down possibilities extremely quickly as the number of situations grows, R2L uses reasoning structures to reduce this growth and avoid the need to explore nearly every possibility.

Ultimately, while traditional RL aims to teach machines how to "think" or reach a goal through trial and error, R2L aims to teach them how to compress uncertainty efficiently.
