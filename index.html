About / Expertise

Private AI systems, built from the workflow back.

Useful AI needs a clear job, a reliable data path, and proof behind each answer.

I build AI systems for teams that need more than a demo. The work starts with the job the system must do.

That job may be:

  • answering from sensitive files with source links
  • reviewing a document packet before a decision
  • running a private LLM inside approved infrastructure
  • testing answers before launch

The common thread is control. I help teams decide what data enters the prompt. I help keep citations attached.

I also help choose where inference runs. That may mean a private VPC, on-prem servers, or a hybrid setup.

My background spans RAG, model serving, ranking, robotics-adjacent AI, and platform reliability. I choose tools around the constraint, not the trend.

The best fit is a regulated, document-heavy, or complex setting. Teams need answers tied to evidence. They also need systems they can monitor after launch.

Why private AI

Why teams choose private AI deployments

Private AI is about control. Teams need to know where data moves, who can see it, and how answers are tested.

50%+

Gartner reported a hard pattern. By the end of 2025, at least half of GenAI projects were abandoned after proof of concept. The causes included poor data, weak risk controls, rising costs, and unclear value.

Gartner analysis
90%

Cisco's 2025 privacy benchmark found that 90% of organizations see local storage as safer. It also found that 64% worry about sharing sensitive data through GenAI tools.

Cisco privacy benchmark
$4.4M

IBM's 2025 breach report lists USD 4.4 million as the global average breach cost. It also says 63% of organizations lacked AI governance policies.

IBM breach report
78%

Stanford HAI's 2025 AI Index reported that organizational AI use rose to 78% in 2024 from 55% in 2023. Adoption is moving beyond experiments.
